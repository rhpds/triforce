== What You'll Learn

Xeon 6 is a flat cost — it doesn't scale with volume. APIs and GPU servers do. The break-even matters.

== Exercise: Walk the Volume Tiers

Open the Triforce demo frontend, navigate to Act 03 (Cost at Scale). Click through the four volume tiers:

[cols="1,1,1,1",options="header"]
|===
| Volume | Xeon 6 | Claude Haiku | Verdict

| 10K/month | $15K/yr | $1K/yr | API wins
| 100K/month | $15K/yr | $10K/yr | Getting close
| 500K/month | $15K/yr | $50K/yr | Xeon wins
| 1M/month | $15K/yr | $101K/yr | Xeon dominates
|===

The Xeon bar doesn't move. Everything else grows linearly.

== Exercise: Find the Break-Even

At what volume does self-hosted beat the cheapest API?

* Claude Haiku: ~$0.0084/record → break-even at ~149K records/month
* Below that: use the API. Above that: Xeon saves $85K-$489K/year.

This is the honest take — at low volume, APIs win. At enterprise scale, the flat line wins.

== The Takeaway

The cost comparison uses sourced pricing: NVIDIA ($3.50/hr H100), AMD ($2.00/hr MI300X), Claude (per-token), Xeon 6 ($15K amortized). The Xeon cost assumes you already own the servers — because most enterprises do.
