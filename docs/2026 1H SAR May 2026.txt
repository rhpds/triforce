A joint Intel–Red Hat initiative focused on enabling open, virtualized, and AI‑enabled RAN solutions using Intel FlexRAN on Red Hat OpenShift. Spans platform enablement, AI integration, validation, and ecosystem readiness to support operator deployments and partner solutions.
Addresses vRAN, OpenRAN, Private 5G RAN, and AI‑RAN across mobile network operators, enterprises, and the RAN ecosystem. Focuses on deployments requiring high performance, scalability, and AI‑driven RAN optimization.
$$$ Market Opportunity
Red Hat: Positions OpenShift as the platform foundation for vRAN and AI‑RAN, increasing relevance with OEMs, ISVs, and operators.
Intel: Drives adoption of Intel platforms, FlexRAN software, and AI acceleration in high‑value RAN infrastructure deployments.
Jointly: Enables ecosystem‑scale RAN adoption through open, validated solutions rather than proprietary stacks.

Secure Access Service Edge (SASE) / NetSec Edge Workstream
Intel – Red Hat Joint Collaboration
Overview & Segments Addressed
A joint Intel–Red Hat initiative focused on enabling and validating secure, cloud-native edge and network security deployments on Red Hat OpenShift, optimized for Intel platforms. Spans platform readiness, validation, ecosystem engagement, and GTM alignment.

The Intel® NetSec Accelerator Reference Design is a reference architecture that enables OEMs, ODMs, and network security vendors to build high-performance PCIe add-in cards that function like autonomous mini-servers dedicated to networking and cybersecurity workloads. 
Targets SASE, network security, and secure edge use cases across service providers, enterprises, and regulated/public-sector environments. Supports deployments where security, performance, and scalability at the edge are critical requirements.
$$$ Market Opportunity
Red Hat: Extends OpenShift adoption into security-led edge and SASE deployments, increasing platform relevance and subscription pull-through in high-value edge environments.
Intel: Drives demand for Intel CPUs and security-centric platforms in edge and network security workloads, which carry strong attach and long lifecycle value.
Jointly: Enables solution-level GTM motions by combining validated platforms, hardware, and security ecosystem partners rather than point technologies.
By offloading these workloads from the host CPU into an isolated execution environment on the accelerator card, the platform improves performance, scalability, workload isolation, power efficiency, and rack-space utilization for edge, enterprise, and cloud deployments. 
Q1/Q2 ’26 Review & Q3/Q4 Outlook

Q1/Q2 Achievements:
Established bi-weekly cadence aligning Intel and Red Hat engineering, product, and GTM teams.
Established Bell Canada as primary anchor customer for SASE / NetSec Edge validation.
Completed PTL1 and PTL2 testing; PTL2 hardware deployed and validated at Bell Canada.
Advanced Arqit ecosystem engagement on OpenShift-based security use cases and public-sector interest.

Q3/Q4 Outlook:
Execute PTL3 validation starting with Bell Canada; extend to Senao once production cards available.
Continue PTL3 with Arqit; enable additional security ecosystem partners.
Leverage Bell Canada outcomes for next customer and OEM expansions (e.g., HPE).
Challenges & Opportunities
What’s left to do:
Complete PTL3 validation and close remaining hardware-dependent items.
Convert Bell Canada learnings into clear GTM artifacts (reference guidance, validation positioning).
Challenges: Hardware availability and readiness (PTL3 cards, server form-factor, edge lab constraints).
Opportunities: Establish Bell Canada as a credible reference deployment and position Intel + Red Hat as a trusted, validated SASE / NetSec Edge platform for broader market adoption.

Edge AI Growing
We are making significant advancements at the edge, including AI, and this journey is only beginning. 
The industry is shifting toward software-defined infrastructure, enabling the consolidation of numerous workloads onto increasingly intelligent nodes. 
This transformation facilitates greater flexibility and scalability via established data center technologies such as containers and virtualization. 
With this newfound flexibility, we have the opportunity to enhance applications, workloads, and workflows with AI, unlocking additional value from data. 
Currently, AI inference is taking a prominent role in real-world applications, and this expand further. 
With the emergence of small language models and large vision models generative AI at the Edge will become mainstream, initially through workflow automation and subsequently through advancements in agentic AI, physical AI, and robotics. 
The future of edge computing is not merely approaching; it is already here and continuously evolving.
