= Explore: Multi-Model Fusion
:navtitle: Multi-Model Fusion

*Time: ~15 minutes* · *Role: Compliance Officer, Clinical Decision Maker*

== What You'll Learn

For critical decisions — compliance checks, diagnostic reasoning, audit-grade outputs — a single model isn't enough. Multi-model fusion sends the same question to 3 models in parallel, then a judge model compares their responses and synthesizes consensus, contradictions, and blind spots.

Cost: ~4x a single call. Value: higher confidence for decisions where being wrong has consequences.

== Exercise 1: Run a Compliance Fusion

[source,bash,role="execute",subs=attributes+]
----
curl -s -X POST {healthcare_url}/api/v1/fusion \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "task": "compliance",
    "prompt": "A customer in Germany transfers $9,500 to a business account in the Cayman Islands. They have made 3 similar transfers in the past month, each just under $10,000. Does this pattern indicate potential structuring under AML regulations? Explain your reasoning."
  }' | jq '{status, panel: .panel.models, judge_model: .judge.model, total_ms, synthesis: .judge.synthesis[:300]}'
----

Three models answer independently. The judge identifies what they agree on, where they differ, and what none of them covered.

== Exercise 2: Compare Single Model vs Fusion

Run the same question through a single model:

[source,bash,role="execute",subs=attributes+]
----
curl -s -X POST {healthcare_url}/api/v1/benchmark/run \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "task": "compliance_reasoning",
    "text": "A customer in Germany transfers $9,500 to a business account in the Cayman Islands. They have made 3 similar transfers in the past month, each just under $10,000.",
    "models": ["granite-2b-cpu"]
  }' | jq '.results[0] | {model, latency_ms, output: .output[:200]}'
----

Compare the depth of reasoning: single model gives one perspective. Fusion gives consensus + contradictions + blind spots.

== Exercise 3: Clinical Diagnostic Fusion

[source,bash,role="execute",subs=attributes+]
----
curl -s -X POST {healthcare_url}/api/v1/fusion \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "task": "diagnosis",
    "prompt": "Patient: 45-year-old female. Symptoms: sudden severe headache, neck stiffness, photophobia, fever 38.9C, altered mental status. Labs: WBC 18,000, CSF elevated protein, low glucose, neutrophilic pleocytosis. What are the top 3 differential diagnoses?"
  }' | jq '{panel_count: .panel.count, panel_latency_ms: .panel.latency_ms, judge_latency_ms: .judge.latency_ms, total_ms, synthesis: .judge.synthesis[:400]}'
----

The judge compares the differential diagnoses from each model — do they all agree on bacterial meningitis? Does any model catch a diagnosis the others missed?

== The Takeaway

Single model: one perspective, one set of blind spots. Fusion panel: three perspectives, judge catches contradictions and gaps. The cost is 4x but for compliance decisions and diagnostic reasoning, the cost of being wrong vastly exceeds the cost of 3 extra inference calls. On CPU at $0/token, the extra calls are literally free.
